Key Points of the Gig Economy

Freelancers undertake specific jobs in return for an agreed-upon payment.

Well-received amongst younger generations.

More flexible working hours and able to work anytime, anywhere in the world.

Requires a certain amount of skills and experiences.

No benefits such as pension funds and health insurance coverage.

Common Problems

User

Planning can be very challenging.

Communication with multiple contractors can be a headache.

Keeping track of budget and payments are manual.

Users need to contact multiple contractors via various sources.

Price can be expensive as there are no competitors in the market.

No information on local market price and costs.



Contractor

Sudden cancellation of order.

Late payments from the client.

Payments normally done through online banking and based solely on trust.

No written contract or insurance guaranteed for the contractors or users.

Difficult for beginner contractors to compete with established or experienced contractors.

Job offers from clients can be very cheap compared to actual local market price.
